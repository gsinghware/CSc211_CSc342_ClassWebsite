Computer Organization LAB.

Description.  Introduction to field programmable logic devices (FPLD) technology, logic synthesis, and rapid prototyping of digital systems using commercial CAD tools. Topics: Programmable Logic Technology. Sequential Design and Hierarchy. Synthesis of Digital Hardware using VHDL. State Machine Design. Moore Machine, Adders and Subtractors, Seven Segment Display, Latches and Flip Flops, Arbitter Circuit, and Encoders and Decoders. Students are required to prepare written reports and demonstrate their design.

Course Outcomes.
     1. Ability to use commercial computer-aided design tools, Quartus to design, simulate, and to perform functional and timing verification         of digital circuits.
     2. Ability to use and to program Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) devices.
     3. Basic Knowledge of VHDL and use of Megacore libraries.
     4. Ability to prepare, present and defend a report that demonstrates correct implementation of the design on a FPGA device.    

Laboratory Projects

# Labs Description
1 Introduction To Quartus This introductory lab allows you to apply everything you learned about the Quartus software.
2 NAND NOR Logic Networks You will design a circuit from a truth table 2 different ways. One using only “NAND” gates, and one using only “NOR” gates.
3 Seven-Segment Display NAND NOR Decoder You will create a circuit that can output to the seven-segment display on the DE2 Board.
4 8-to-4 Priority Encoder You will create a circuit that will be able to alphabetize the output of your circuit from lab 3 by creating several priority encoders and a multiplexer.
5 Flip-Flops, Latches, and SRAM Part 1: You will design latches, flip flops, and a shift register. Part 2: You will design an 8x4 SRAM.
6 Sequence Detector You will design a Moore machine that is capable of detecting a sequence of bits.
7 Arbiter Circuit You will design an arbiter circuit that allows you to use multiple circuits with a single shared resource.
8 Adders You will design several adders.

Grading Policy.  To get a 100% grade in the lab All projects have to be completed + Detailed reports. Proper reporting is essential to get a 100% grade. Each report should contain the following sections: Objective, Source code, Explanations and Screen shots + Pictures of the DE-2 board with the display, Conclusions. The display should demonstrate that your design works on the board! If your report is not in a proper form and it does not prove that your design works on the board - you will get partial credit (reduced grade).
