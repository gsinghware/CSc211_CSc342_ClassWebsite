Computer Organization LAB.

Description.  Introduction to field programmable logic devices (FPLD) technology, logic synthesis, and rapid prototyping of digital systems using commercial CAD tools. Topics: Programmable Logic Technology. Sequential Design and Hierarchy. Synthesis of Digital Hardware using VHDL. State Machine Design, CPU Controller. A Simple Processor Design. Video Graphics Adapter(VGA) video display generation. Design PS/2 Keyboard interface. Design of PS/2 Mouse interface. Synthesis of a RISC processor as covered in CSc 34200. Students are required to prepare written reports and demonstrate their design.

Course Outcomes.
     1. Ability to use commercial computer-aided design tools ModelSim, Quartus to design, simulate, and to perform functional and          timing verification of digital circuits.
     2. Ability to use and to program Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) devices.
     3. Basic Knowledge of VHDL and use of Megacore libraries.
     4. Ability to prepare, present and defend a report that demonstrates correct implementation of the design on a FPGA device.    

Laboratory Projects

# Labs Description
1 Design, Simulation and Testing of One-Bit Comparator You will learn how to use the ModelSim Software and design a comparator circuit.
2 Adders, Multiplexers You will design an adder in both VHDL and Quartus. You will also test this design on the DE2 board.
3 Latches and FlipFlops You will design latches, flip-flops, and registers.
4 Finite State machines You will implement 3-digit BCD counter using real-time clock.
5 Registers  
6 Memory You will use as megacore memory block to store and retrieve values.
7 Keyboard You will understand the ps2 communication protocol and the operation of a ps2 keyboard.
8 VGA You will understand how video is displayed on a monitor using a VGA connection.
9 Simple Processor You will design a simple processor.
10 Enhanced Processor  

Grading Policy.  To get a 100% grade in the lab All projects have to be completed + Detailed reports. Proper reporting is essential to get a 100% grade. Each report should contain the following sections: Objective, Source code, Explanations and Screen shots + Pictures of the DE-2 board with the display, Conclusions. The display should demonstrate that your design works on the board! If your report is not in a proper form and it does not prove that your design works on the board - you will get partial credit (reduced grade).
